Platform · Webhook Relay
01 · Overview

One relay, so no team talks to Stripe directly

webhook-relay is a small Node service that receives Stripe's webhook stream once, verifies it, dedupes it in Postgres, and republishes it to internal consumers over SQS.

PurposeGive every internal service one deduped, signed feed of Stripe events, so no team builds its own Stripe webhook endpoint.
BoundaryThe relay owns receiving, verifying, deduping, and publishing to SQS. It does not own what a consumer does with an event, and it never calls back into Stripe.
SuccessEvery Stripe event reaches every subscribed consumer at least once, and a consumer that dedupes on event.id sees zero duplicate side effects.
02 · Key Decisions

At-least-once, 7-day retention, dead-letter after 5

Three decisions shape everything else in this plan. None of them are reversible without changing how consumer teams write their handlers, so they're fixed here before implementation starts.

DecisionChoiceWhy
Delivery guaranteeAt-least-onceExactly-once needs a distributed transaction across Stripe, Postgres, and SQS. Every extra guarantee is a new failure mode. Consumers absorb duplicates instead.
Event retention7 days in PostgresCovers Stripe's own 3-day automatic retry window with margin, and gives on-call a real window to replay events by hand if a consumer ships a bug.
Retry ceiling5 attempts, then dead-letterLong enough to survive a deploy or a short outage. Short enough that a broken consumer gets flagged in minutes, not days.
Fanout transportOne SQS queue per consumerIsolates a slow or broken consumer from every other one. No consumer's backlog can slow another consumer's queue.
Decision
At-least-once is the one fact every consumer team needs before writing a handler: dedupe on event.id, or expect to eventually double-process a payment event.
03 · Architecture

Stripe never talks to a consumer directly

Stripe posts to exactly one endpoint. The relay verifies, dedupes against Postgres, and republishes to one SQS queue per consumer. A consumer only ever talks to its own queue, never to Stripe and never to another consumer's queue.

  1. Ingress. Stripe posts every event to one HTTPS endpoint. The relay checks the Stripe-Signature header and a 5-minute timestamp window before anything else happens.
  2. Core. A Postgres unique constraint on the Stripe event id is the only source of truth for "have we seen this before." First-seen events get published; repeats get acknowledged and dropped.
  3. Fanout. Every subscribed consumer owns one SQS queue. The relay publishes once per queue, so a stuck consumer never slows down the others.
04 · Workflow

Four steps from Stripe POST to consumer receipt

The path is short on purpose: verify, dedupe, publish, and (only on failure) retry. Every step either finishes in milliseconds or hands off to SQS, so the HTTP handler that talks to Stripe never blocks on a consumer.

  1. Receive & verify. Validate the Stripe-Signature header against the webhook secret and a 5-minute timestamp tolerance. A bad signature gets a 400 and never reaches Postgres.
  2. Dedupe. Insert the event id into a unique-indexed table. If the insert conflicts, the event was already handled; the relay returns 200 immediately and skips fanout.
  3. Publish. On a first-seen insert, publish one message per subscriber queue in SQS, each tagged with the event id and attempt: 1.
  4. Retry or park. A failed consumer delivery goes back on its queue with backoff (30s, 1m, 2m, 4m, 8m). After 5 total attempts, SQS moves the message to that consumer's dead-letter queue and pages on-call.

Contract surface

# the relay's own endpoints, not Stripe's
POST /webhooks/stripe          Stripe    -> relay, verified + deduped
POST /events/:id/replay        On-call   -> relay, re-publish one event to every queue
GET  /events/:id               On-call   -> relay, inspect delivery + attempt history
05 · Rollout

Three phases, each provable before the next starts

#PhaseDeliversMeasured by
1Shadow modeRelay verifies, dedupes, and stores every Stripe event, but publishes to no consumer queue.Zero missed or malformed events over 2 weeks, checked against Stripe's own event log.
2Single consumerFanout turns on for exactly one low-risk consumer; every other team keeps its current direct Stripe integration.POST-to-delivery latency and duplicate rate for that one consumer, watched for 1 to 2 weeks.
3All consumersEvery remaining consumer cuts over to the relay; direct Stripe webhook endpoints elsewhere get deleted.100% of consumers on the relay, zero direct Stripe subscriptions left outside it.
Verify first
Before shadow mode starts: the Stripe webhook secret is rotated into the secrets manager, the phase-2 pilot consumer's team has agreed to dedupe on event.id, and the dead-letter queue alarm pages on-call, not a dashboard nobody watches.
